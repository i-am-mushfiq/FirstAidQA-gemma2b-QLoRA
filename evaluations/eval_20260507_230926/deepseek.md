### KEY FINDINGS
- **Catastrophic infant choking management (Q21): all seven variants gave lethally dangerous advice**, recommending lateral positioning or abdominal thrusts, instead of the correct back blows and chest thrusts.
- **Systematic failure in shock positioning (Q17): every model incorrectly placed a conscious shock patient in the lateral recovery position** rather than supine with leg elevation.
- **Calling emergency services is frequently omitted** even in life-threatening scenarios such as cardiac arrest, drowning, and severe bleeding.
- **Spinal injury protection is unreliable** – half of the models would remove a helmet or move a spinal patient without log-roll precautions, risking paralysis or death.
- **Embedded glass management (Q22) is uniformly flawed**, with all variants advising direct pressure on the object rather than around it.
